Tutorial of my life

1Carl’s childhood began with curiosity and a tendency to wander both physically and mentally. 2One defining moment occurred when he got lost on a crowded beach as a young child. 3He walked along the shore for nearly half an hour, calling out for his parents. 4Although fear slowly crept in, he refused to stop moving. 5Instead of breaking down, he observed the world around him. 6During that search, Carl noticed a small fish stranded on the wet sand. 7The fish struggled to breathe, which immediately caught Carl’s attention. 8Feeling sympathy, he carefully picked it up with his small hands. 9He walked toward the waves and released the fish back into the ocean. 10Watching it swim away filled him with pride and relief. 11Not long after, his father finally found him. 12His father’s reaction was a mix of fear and overwhelming relief. 13Carl, however, appeared calm and unfazed. 14He was covered in sand from running around the beach. 15As a child, he was playful and energetic. 16That playful nature sometimes led to trouble at school. 17Once, he made a classmate cry by throwing a pencil case too hard. 18Despite mistakes, Carl always showed kindness afterward. 19He had a good heart and strong imagination. 20Those early experiences shaped his sense of empathy and creativity.

Mastered the controls

1During his teenage years, Carl became a restless explorer of interests. 2He joined multiple sports teams to see where he might belong. 3Basketball was the first sport he seriously tried. 4Although he enjoyed it, the passion did not last. 5He later attempted volleyball but felt disconnected from it. 6Music became another area of exploration. 7Carl learned to play several instruments during this time. 8He practiced guitar and gradually improved his skills. 9Despite progress, he felt it was still not enough. 10Drawing also interested him for a short period. 11However, inconsistency prevented mastery. 12Carl continued moving from one hobby to another. 13This pattern made him question his abilities. 14Others described him as a jack of all trades. 15While he accepted the label, it caused self-doubt. 16He wondered if he would ever find his true calling. 17Despite confusion, he never stopped trying new things. 18His imagination remained active throughout these years. 19He constantly created stories in his head. 20Those imaginative thoughts quietly prepared him for his future path.

Heavier difficulty

1College marked a turning point in Carl’s life. 2He finally pursued Computer Science, a long-held dream. 3At the beginning, he felt motivated and confident. 4As lessons progressed, the difficulty increased. 5The workload demanded discipline and time management. 6Carl struggled to keep up during his first year. 7Despite his effort, he failed one subject. 8The failure deeply frustrated him. 9However, it also humbled him. 10He realized effort alone was not enough. 11Instead of quitting, he chose to retake the subject. 12He promised himself to improve. 13Carl began managing his time more carefully. 14He practiced more consistently. 15He also learned to ask for help when needed. 16This mindset shift strengthened him. 17Failure became a lesson rather than an ending. 18College slowly matured his character. 19His commitment to his dream grew stronger. 20Above all, Carl focused on becoming the best version of himself.
